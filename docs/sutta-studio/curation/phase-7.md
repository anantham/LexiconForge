# Phase 7 — yadidaṁ cattāro satipaṭṭhānā

> "Namely, the four foundations of mindfulness" — bridge between the formula-of-purposes
> (phases 2-6) and the listing of the four foundations. This is the sutta naming itself.
> Curated 2026-05-14, post-phantom-purge.

## Time

~9 min — under the formula-chain phases. Less doctrinal density per word
(yadidaṁ is just a discourse marker, cattāro is just a number). The
satipaṭṭhānā compound carried the curatorial weight.

## Why this phase matters

This is where MN10 says its own title. "yadidaṁ cattāro satipaṭṭhānā" =
"namely, the four foundations of mindfulness" — the sutta is called
Satipaṭṭhāna Sutta precisely because this phrase appears.

Phase 7 also signals a grammatical shift: phases 2-6 used dative-of-purpose
suffixes (-āya, -yā) to enumerate WHY the practice exists. Phase 7 uses
NOMINATIVE plural (-o, -ā) to enumerate WHAT the practice consists of.
Purpose-clauses give way to substance.

## The contested compound

Satipaṭṭhāna admits two morphological analyses:

1. **sati + paṭṭhāna** — paṭi + √sthā, "standing-against" or "foundation".
   Reading: foundations of mindfulness.
2. **sati + upaṭṭhāna** — upa + √sthā, "standing-close-to" or "presencing".
   Reading: establishments of mindfulness.

The consonant assimilation (tt) masks which prefix was originally there.
Bhikkhu Anālayo's monograph *Satipaṭṭhāna: The Direct Path to Realization*
defends the second parse philologically.

Three translator schools:
- Bhikkhu Bodhi: "foundations" (paṭṭhāna parse)
- Sujato: "establishments" / "kinds of meditation" (upaṭṭhāna parse)
- Thanissaro: "frames of reference" (cognitive-structures reading)

This is exactly the kind of contested-compound moment where the
F task (translator-tradition DB) would harden the curator's claims.

## What this phase teaches about the formula structure

The transition from -āya (dative purpose) to -o/-ā (nominative listing) is
the formula's structural pivot point. The "for-the-sake-of" phrases ended
with phase-6 (sacchikiriyāya). Phase-7 begins the "the X consist of" phase.

This is the architecture of the sutta itself, made audible through
case-marking. The Pāli grammar tells the reader: "I just told you WHY;
now I will tell you WHAT."
