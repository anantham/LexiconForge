## Concerns flagged

### Translator citations

I claimed:
- Bhikkhu Bodhi: "method" / "attainment"
- Sujato: "system" / "realizing"
- Ñāṇamoli: "True Way" (commentarial)
- Thanissaro: "discovery"

These claims come from training-memory familiarity, not from a verified
tradition database. I'm ~80-85% confident on Bodhi and Sujato in MN10's
published translations. Less confident on the specific Thanissaro
attribution for adhigama → "discovery" — the framing is consistent with
his theological style but I haven't verified the exact word-choice for
this verse. F task (translator-tradition DB) would harden these.

### ñāya is genuinely contested

Buddhaghosa reading (ñāya = Noble Eightfold Path) is the strongest doctrinal
reading and is what makes this phrase the structural pivot of the formula
("for attaining the path" frames the WHOLE practice). Sujato's "system"
preserves the technical-procedural flavor but loses the soteriological
weight. Bodhi's "method" splits the difference.

Three published English translations sit on three different readings of
the same word. The reader sees all three; that surfaces the disagreement
honestly without picking a winner.

## What this phase teaches about the formula

The grammatical pivot — from -ānaṁ (genitive plural) to -ssa (genitive
singular) — is the conceptual pivot. Phases 2-4 named what the practice
purifies/overcomes/extinguishes: beings, griefs, sufferings. All plural
because the WORLD is plural. Phase 5 and phase 6 name what the practice
arrives at: the path, nibbāna. Both singular because the GOAL is one.

The Pāli vocabulary scaffolding makes this shift legible without naming
it. The reader hears the grammar tighten.

This is the kind of cross-phase observation v11 cannot make — it sees only
phase-5 in its prompt window. The curator's value-add is precisely this:
seeing what only emerges when the chain is read as a whole.
