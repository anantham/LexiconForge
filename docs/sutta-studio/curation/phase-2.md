## Context

Phase 2 sits inside MN10's opening formula at canonical segment `mn10:2.1`:

```
mn10:2.1   ekāyano ayaṁ bhikkhave maggo sattānaṁ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṁ samatikkamāya
            └──── phase-1 ───┘└─2─┘└──────── phases 3-5 ─────────────────────┘
```

The bilara segment is sub-segmented by the compiler's skeleton pass into multiple study phases
via `wordRange`. Phase 2 holds two content words: **sattānaṁ** (genitive plural, "of beings")
and **visuddhiyā** (dative singular feminine, "for purification").

This is the first phase of the formula where the Buddha names WHO the path is for and WHAT
it's for. Pedagogically critical — establishes the path's beneficiaries (broader than the
monastic audience listening) and the path's first stated purpose (purification, the most-cited
of the formula's five "for"s).

## Curation decisions

### p5 sattānaṁ ("of beings")

**Segmentation** — unchanged from v10: satt (root) + ānaṁ (suffix). The compound parse is
uncontroversial; v10 got this right.

**morph field added on p5s2**: `{ case: 'gen', number: 'pl' }`. v10 lacked this. The Pāli
morphology generator (DPD POS tag → morph) could supply this deterministically — flagging
as a candidate for **A3 post-pass #1 (morph-from-POS)**.

**Tooltips rewritten in §3.4 plain-first prose**:
- v10 had `[Genitive Plural] Of beings` + `🔗 Shows who it belongs to` — bracketed grammar
  prefix + decorative emoji.
- v11 replacement: full sentence explaining what the -ānaṁ ending DOES and why it's encoded
  on the noun (because Pāli marks "belongs-to" on the possessor noun, where English uses the
  preposition "of"). This is the pay-rent rule at work: the term "genitive plural" appears
  in the tooltip but is glossed in the same sentence.
- Added a cross-phase note: same -ānaṁ suffix recurs in phase-3 on sokaparideva-ānaṁ. Helps
  readers see the pattern across the formula.

**Senses (3, with epistemicBasis + confidence + notes)**:
- `beings` — lexical, high, DPD-attested. The default.
- `the attached` — etymological, low, from alternate root √saj 'to cling'. Pedagogically
  interesting because it captures the Buddhist analysis (beings ARE the attached ones) but
  rarely chosen as primary rendering.
- `the seven` — etymological, low, homonym. NOT the intended reading, but included to
  illustrate Pāli's lexical polysemy. Students will encounter satta meaning seven elsewhere.

The v10 had similar 3-sense polysemy but no metadata. The CONTENT of the senses is mostly
right in v10; the metadata is the V2 addition.

**Relation kept**: p5s2 → p6, ownership, "Purification OF". This **earns its arrow** per
the arrow-earning rule — Pāli marks the genitive-of-possession on the noun where English
uses 'of' as a preposition. The case-quirk is real; the arrow is pedagogically valuable.

**Cross-phase tooltip on p5s1** introduces the sattā-vs-bhikkhū contrast (the Buddha is
addressing monks in phases e-g, but the path is FOR all beings, sattā). This is the cross-
phase awareness amendment in action — references prior recurring lemmas. v10 had no such
awareness.

### p6 visuddhiyā ("for purification")

**Segmentation** — unchanged from v10: vi (prefix) + suddhi (root) + yā (suffix). All three
elements are pedagogically informative; the v10 parse was already correct.

**morph field added on p6s3**: `{ case: 'dat', number: 'sg', gender: 'f' }`. v10 lacked
this. Note the gender field — visuddhi is feminine (Pāli abstract nouns in -i are typically
feminine). The hand curation includes gender because it justifies the -yā vs -ya ending
distinction. **A3 post-pass #1 candidate.**

**isAnchor: true** on p6. Anchor selection per V2 amendment §4: pick the semantic centerpiece.
For phase-2, that's visuddhiyā — the WHOLE phase is about WHAT THE PATH IS FOR. sattānaṁ
modifies it (the beneficiaries of the purification) but visuddhi is the head noun. v10 had
no anchor at all.

**Tooltips rewritten** — drop `[Prefix] Intensive / completely` + `√sudh: Purity, brightness`
+ `[Dative] For the sake of` and the emojis (`✨`, `🎯`). Replace with plain-first prose:
- p6s1 vi-: explains the productive prefix in vipassanā / viññāṇa — cross-references that
  students will see often.
- p6s2 suddhi: includes the Sanskrit cognate (śuddha) for students with that background;
  notes that Pāli abstract nouns straddle state/process (relevant for the "purification vs
  purity" sense choice below).
- p6s3 -yā: explains dative-of-purpose; cross-references phase-3's samatikkamāya as the
  next dative-of-purpose in the formula.

**Senses expanded to 6, all with curatorial epistemicBasis + per-tradition notes**:

| Rendering | Confidence | Cited tradition |
|---|---|---|
| purification | high | Sujato, Ñāṇamoli-Bodhi (the process reading; most modern) |
| purity | high | Thanissaro (the state reading; equally defensible) |
| clarity | medium | Insight-tradition reading; connects to vipassanā |
| cleansing | medium | Ritual-water metaphor; concrete physicality |
| brightening | low | Luminosity metaphor; less canonical |
| refinement | low | Process-metaphor (refining gold); modern |

v10 had 5 senses already (purification, clarity, cleansing, brightening, refinement) — solid
polysemy. v11 hand-curation:
- Adds the "purity" reading (state vs process — explicitly attributed to Thanissaro)
- Reorders to put the two high-confidence renderings first
- Adds confidence rankings
- Adds tradition notes for each

This was the famously-contested-word workflow we documented in V2_TRANSLATOR_DEBATE.
visuddhi is less contested than ekāyano (phase-1 has 5 senses for one word), but it still
has the state-vs-process tension that's worth flagging.

| Relations + arrow-earning | v10 has the genitive arrow correctly | likely retained | retained |

If these predictions hold, v11 is at ~65% of hand-quality. The gaps that A3 post-passes
could close:
1. **morph-from-POS** — close to 100% if DPD's POS tag is queried per word and morph is
   filled deterministically. Closes the morph gap entirely.
2. **citation-linker** — validate any LLM-supplied sourceCitationIds against the DPD lookups
   already in the prompt context; strip invalid ones. Closes the hallucination risk.
3. **anchor-selector** — if v11 didn't set isAnchor, a heuristic post-pass picks the
   semantic-center word (longest content word? main noun?) and sets the flag.
4. **§3.4 linter** — flag any bracketed grammar prefix `[X Y]` or emoji-default `🔗/✨/🎯`
   that slips through; report to curator for manual cleanup OR auto-rewrite.

The gaps post-passes CAN'T close:
- Translator-tradition citations (needs F: translator-tradition DB — separate work)
- Cross-phase awareness richness (needs phaseState envelope plumbing into the compiler call —
  partially in place but not always used)
- The state-vs-process editorial choice on visuddhi senses (genuinely curatorial judgment)

## Open issues

- **DPD citation IDs**: the hand-curation doesn't populate `sourceCitationIds` because I
  don't have the DPD database loaded in this session to look up the canonical citation IDs
  for sattā / visuddhi. The schema permits the field; populating it is candidate work for
  the citation-linker post-pass OR a follow-up commit once DPD is queried.
- **Refrain detection**: sattā is NOT a refrain word here (appears once in MN10's opening,
  not part of the contemplation-formula refrains that recur 9× across the body). No
  `refrainId` needed. visuddhi could plausibly be tagged with a `purpose-words` refrain
  family (along with samatikkamāya, atthaṅgamāya, adhigamāya, sacchikiriyāya in phases 3-5) —
  deferred until the broader refrain-tagging pass.
